Secure Systems Architecture

19th of September - 30th October

Discussion 1: Modeling Languages

In software and systems engineering, UML is adept at detailing both static and dynamic software system aspects, whereas SysML extends UML's capabilities to model more complex systems that may include hardware, software, and processes. UML is suited for software-focused initiatives, while SysML addresses integrated systems projects that require a broader scope.

Discussion 2: Cybersecurity Hard Problems

The NSA's Science of Security Symposium highlights pressing challenges in cybersecurity. We examine "A comprehensive science of security for ultra-large-scale systems," emphasizing both modeling-based solutions like formal verification and security models, as well as non-modeling approaches such as scalable architectures and component reusability.
